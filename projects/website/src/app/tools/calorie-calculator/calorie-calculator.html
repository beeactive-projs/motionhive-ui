Understanding TDEE for active individuals

Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE) represents the cumulative calories your body burns in a 24-hour period. For active individuals, TDEE varies significantly depending on daily exertion levels. While the Mifflin-St Jeor equation serves as the primary benchmark for estimation, you should refine your target by 100–200 kcal after two weeks based on real-world weight data.

Why macros matter for recovery

Hitting your calorie target is the first lever — but the macro split determines whether those calories rebuild muscle, replenish glycogen, or support hormonal balance. A 40 / 30 / 30 distribution (carbs / protein / fat) works well for most group fitness athletes. Adjust towards more protein (up to 35–40%) if your primary goal is body recomposition.
